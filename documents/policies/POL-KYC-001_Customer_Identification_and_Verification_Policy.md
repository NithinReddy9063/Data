# Customer Identification and Verification Policy

## 1 Purpose

This policy defines the mandatory requirements for identifying and verifying customers of APEX Commercial Bank before a product, account, service, facility, or transaction relationship is activated. It ensures that customer identity evidence is collected, validated, risk assessed, approved, and retained in a consistent manner across ACB.

The policy supports the ACB requirement that customer identification and verification controls are applied before standard account opening, high risk review, payment enablement, lending assessment, trade finance processing, or corporate banking service activation. It also establishes the minimum decision rules used by Operations Officers, Operations Managers, Relationship Managers, Relationship Officers, Compliance Officers, Senior Compliance Officers, AML Analysts, Credit Risk Officers, Branch Managers, Regional Managers, and Department Heads when handling customer identity evidence.

## 2 Scope

This policy applies to all approved ACB products and all approved customer types listed in the Document Control section. It applies at initial onboarding, material customer data change, periodic KYC review, reactivation of dormant customer relationships, and any event where previously accepted identity evidence is found to be incomplete, expired, inconsistent, or not retrievable.

This policy applies to the following activities:

| Activity | Applicability |
| --- | --- |
| Customer identity capture | Required before customer relationship activation |
| Address verification | Required where address evidence is part of the applicable KYC document set |
| Non-individual constitution verification | Required for Sole Proprietor, Partnership, Private Limited, Public Limited, Trust, Government Entity, Financial Institution, and NGO customers |
| Beneficial ownership verification | Required where ownership, control, or authorized signatory evidence is applicable |
| Risk-based escalation | Required for Medium, High, and Critical risk conditions |
| Record retention | Required for all identification and verification evidence |

This policy does not replace product-specific credit, payment, trade finance, or service processing controls. Those controls must be read together with this policy where customer identification or verification evidence is required.

## 3 Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Identification | The capture of customer information required to establish the identity of an individual or non-individual customer. |
| Customer Verification | The validation of customer identity information against acceptable documentary evidence, system records, or approved review evidence. |
| Customer Due Diligence | The review performed to understand the customer profile, customer type, product purpose, risk level, and required evidence. |
| Enhanced Due Diligence | Additional review required for High or Critical risk customers, incomplete ownership structures, material adverse indicators, or other elevated risk conditions. |
| Identity Evidence | A valid document or approved record used to confirm customer identity. |
| Address Evidence | A valid document or approved record used to confirm a customer's residential, registered, communication, or operating address. |
| Beneficial Ownership | Ownership or control information required for non-individual customers where an individual or controlling party must be identified and verified. |
| Material Mismatch | A discrepancy that affects customer identity, customer type, ownership, authority, address, or risk classification. |
| KYC Record | The complete customer identification, verification, approval, exception, and retention record maintained by ACB. |

## 4 Policy Statements

1. ACB must identify and verify every customer before activating any approved product or service relationship.
2. No account, payment service, lending facility, trade finance service, or corporate banking arrangement may be activated when mandatory identity evidence is missing, expired, illegible, or materially inconsistent.
3. Customer identification must use only approved customer types and approved products from ACB-KB-BIBLE-001.
4. The Operations Officer must verify that the customer record contains the required evidence before forwarding the case for approval or activation.
5. The Operations Manager must review exceptions, evidence gaps, and unresolved mismatches before the case proceeds beyond operations control.
6. Relationship Managers and Relationship Officers may support customer information collection but must not override document verification controls.
7. Senior Compliance Officer review is required for High risk customer verification decisions and any case involving unresolved identity, address, beneficial ownership, or sanctions-related concerns.
8. Critical risk cases must follow the approved escalation hierarchy and require Chief Compliance Officer approval before relationship activation.
9. Customer verification decisions must be supported by a documented decision rationale in the KYC Record.
10. All accepted and rejected identity evidence must be retained in a retrievable format according to the applicable KYC record maintenance SOP and related KOD requirements.
11. Where customer identification evidence is insufficient, ACB must either obtain corrective evidence, apply an approved exception, escalate the case, or decline activation.
12. Verification activities must meet the ACB SLA standard of 4 Hours for Document Verification unless an approved exception or escalation condition applies.

## 5 Roles

| Role | Policy Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Relationship Manager | Ensures customer-facing information collection is complete for assigned relationships and routes KYC evidence to operations without altering verification outcomes. |
| Relationship Officer | Supports customer communication, obtains missing information requested by operations, and records customer responses in the onboarding or servicing workflow. |
| Operations Officer | Performs first-level document verification, confirms customer type, checks mandatory evidence, records verification results, and identifies mismatches. |
| Operations Manager | Reviews operational exceptions, approves standard remediation paths, confirms SLA adherence, and escalates unresolved or higher risk cases. |
| Compliance Officer | Reviews KYC control adherence, validates compliance concerns, and advises on policy interpretation for standard and Medium risk cases. |
| Senior Compliance Officer | Provides compliance oversight for High risk cases, unresolved identity concerns, and enhanced due diligence requirements. |
| AML Analyst | Reviews AML-related indicators identified during customer verification and supports escalation for suspicious or sanctions-related concerns. |
| Credit Risk Officer | Confirms that customer identification evidence used for lending products is consistent with credit assessment records. |
| Branch Manager | Reviews branch-level escalations and ensures customer activation does not proceed where mandatory verification controls remain open. |
| Regional Manager | Reviews escalations above Branch Manager authority and ensures consistent application across branches. |
| Department Head | Owns this policy, ensures annual review, and confirms that operational procedures remain aligned to the approved KYC control framework. |

## 6 Decision Rules

### 6.1 Customer Verification Decision Table

| Condition | Risk Level | Decision | Minimum Approval | Required Record |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| All mandatory identity and address evidence is valid, legible, current, and consistent | Low | Accept verification and proceed to applicable SOP workflow | Operations Officer | Verified identity checklist and accepted evidence record |
| Minor clerical difference is present and does not affect identity, customer type, ownership, authority, or address | Medium | Proceed only after correction is recorded and reviewed | Operations Manager | Correction note, corrected customer record, and evidence comparison |
| Identity evidence is expired, illegible, incomplete, or not customer-linked | High | Do not activate relationship until valid evidence is obtained or an approved exception is recorded | Senior Compliance Officer | Rejection reason, customer communication record, and replacement evidence request |
| Material mismatch exists in customer name, date of birth, registered name, address, ownership, or authority | High | Suspend activation and complete enhanced review | Senior Compliance Officer | Mismatch assessment, customer explanation, supporting evidence, and decision rationale |
| Identity concern is linked to suspicious activity, sanctions screening concern, or unresolved ownership opacity | Critical | Escalate and do not activate without executive approval | Chief Compliance Officer | Escalation record, AML review note, and final approval decision |

### 6.2 Customer Type Evidence Decision Table

| Customer Type | Minimum Verification Focus | Decision Rule | Escalation Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Individual | Identity, address, date of birth, and customer contact details | Accept only where identity and address evidence are consistent with the customer record | Material identity or address mismatch |
| Minor | Minor identity evidence and authorized guardian evidence | Accept only where guardian authority is documented and linked to the minor record | Missing guardian evidence or conflicting authority |
| NRI | Identity, overseas or domestic address evidence, and residency status evidence | Accept only where NRI status is supported by valid evidence | Inconsistent residency status or expired evidence |
| Resident | Identity and address evidence | Accept where evidence is valid and aligned to the customer record | Address mismatch not resolved within Document Verification SLA |
| Sole Proprietor | Proprietor identity and business constitution evidence | Accept only where proprietor and business records are linked | Business name mismatch or unverified proprietor identity |
| Partnership | Constitution evidence, partner identity, and authority evidence | Accept only where partner authority is documented | Missing partner authority or ownership ambiguity |
| Private Limited | Constitution evidence, authorized signatory evidence, and ownership evidence | Accept only where company and signatory records are consistent | Missing beneficial ownership evidence |
| Public Limited | Constitution evidence and authorized signatory evidence | Accept only where signatory authority is confirmed | Conflicting board or authority evidence |
| Trust | Trust constitution and authorized trustee evidence | Accept only where trustee authority is documented | Missing trust deed or trustee authority concern |
| Government Entity | Entity evidence and authorized official evidence | Accept only where official authority is recorded | Unclear official authority or missing government evidence |
| Financial Institution | Institution evidence, authorization evidence, and enhanced review evidence where required | Accept only after risk level is confirmed | High or Critical risk classification |
| NGO | Constitution evidence, authorized signatory evidence, and purpose evidence where required | Accept only where registration and authority evidence align | Missing registration evidence or purpose concern |

### 6.3 SLA and Escalation Decision Table

| Event | SLA | Action Required | Escalation Path |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Standard document verification request received | 4 Hours | Operations Officer completes verification or records blocker | Operations Manager |
| Standard account opening is dependent on completed KYC | 2 Business Days | Operations Manager ensures KYC decision supports account opening SLA | Branch Manager |
| High risk customer review is required | 5 Business Days | Senior Compliance Officer completes enhanced review or records escalation reason | Regional Manager |
| AML concern is identified during verification | 3 Business Days | AML Analyst reviews and records AML investigation outcome | Department Head |
| Critical risk decision is required | 5 Business Days unless restricted by AML investigation requirements | Activation remains suspended until approval is recorded | Chief Compliance Officer |

## 7 Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy are permitted only when the exception is documented, risk assessed, approved at the required level, and cross-referenced to the applicable Exception Matrix.

| Exception Scenario | Permitted Decision | Approval Required | Related Exception Matrix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Temporary unavailability of address evidence where identity is otherwise verified | Conditional continuation of onboarding without activation until address evidence is received | Operations Manager | [EXM-KYC-002](../exception_matrices/EXM-KYC-002_Address_Proof_Exception_Matrix.md) |
| Minor clerical variation in customer name where supporting evidence confirms the same customer | Correct record and proceed after review | Operations Manager | [EXM-KYC-001](../exception_matrices/EXM-KYC-001_Identity_Document_Exception_Matrix.md) |
| Beneficial ownership evidence is incomplete for a non-individual customer | Suspend activation pending enhanced review | Senior Compliance Officer | [EXM-KYC-003](../exception_matrices/EXM-KYC-003_Beneficial_Ownership_Exception_Matrix.md) |
| Customer classified as Critical risk due to unresolved identity or ownership concern | Do not activate unless executive approval is recorded | Chief Compliance Officer | [EXM-KYC-003](../exception_matrices/EXM-KYC-003_Beneficial_Ownership_Exception_Matrix.md) |
| Identity evidence cannot be validated within the Document Verification SLA | Escalate for operational review and customer communication | Branch Manager | [EXM-KYC-001](../exception_matrices/EXM-KYC-001_Identity_Document_Exception_Matrix.md) |

An exception must never be used to bypass sanctions screening, AML investigation requirements, beneficial ownership verification for applicable non-individual customers, or executive approval for Critical risk cases.

## 8 Escalation

Escalation must follow the approved ACB approval hierarchy. Each escalation must include the customer type, product requested, risk level, evidence gap, operational impact, recommended decision, and supporting documents.

| Escalation Level | Escalation Trigger | Expected Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Operations Officer | Initial verification cannot be completed because evidence is missing, expired, illegible, or inconsistent | Record blocker, request remediation, and route to Operations Manager |
| Operations Manager | Evidence issue cannot be resolved within standard verification handling or requires an operational exception | Review exception path, confirm SLA impact, and approve or escalate |
| Branch Manager | Customer activation is blocked and customer-facing impact requires branch-level decision | Confirm no activation occurs until KYC control is satisfied or approved exception exists |
| Regional Manager | Repeated or material branch-level KYC control issues occur | Review consistency of decisioning and escalate unresolved risk concerns |
| Department Head | Policy interpretation, systemic KYC evidence issue, or control failure is identified | Determine corrective action and coordinate compliance review |
| Compliance Head | Material compliance concern or High risk control issue requires senior compliance review | Confirm compliance position and route Critical risk matters |
| Chief Compliance Officer | Critical risk decision or executive approval is required | Approve, reject, or impose conditions on the customer activation decision |

## 9 Controls

| Control ID | Control Name | Control Objective | Performer | Frequency | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-001 | Mandatory Evidence Completeness Check | Ensure required identification evidence is present before activation | Operations Officer | Per customer event | Completed verification checklist and customer evidence record |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-002 | Identity Consistency Review | Detect material mismatches across identity, address, ownership, and authority evidence | Operations Officer | Per customer event | Evidence comparison note and mismatch decision record |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-003 | Exception Approval Review | Ensure exceptions are approved at the required level and mapped to the correct Exception Matrix | Operations Manager | Per exception | Exception approval record and EXM reference |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-004 | High Risk Verification Oversight | Ensure High risk identification concerns receive enhanced compliance review | Senior Compliance Officer | Per High risk case | Enhanced review note and approval outcome |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-005 | Critical Risk Activation Hold | Prevent activation of Critical risk customer relationships without executive approval | Branch Manager | Per Critical risk case | Activation hold record and Chief Compliance Officer decision |
| KYC-CIV-CTRL-006 | KYC Record Retention Review | Confirm accepted and rejected evidence is retrievable for audit and review | Department Head | Quarterly | KYC record sample review and remediation tracker |

Control failures must be recorded by the Operations Manager and escalated according to the Escalation section. Any repeat control failure affecting customer activation, evidence retention, or High or Critical risk decisioning must be reviewed by the Department Head.
